## Radio Basics for Beginners

### What is Traffic/Log Management?

Think of a radio station like a restaurant:
- **The Menu** = Your music and shows
- **The Kitchen** = Your DJs and announcers  
- **The Orders** = Advertisements and commercials
- **Traffic Management** = Making sure everything happens at the right time

**LibreLog is your scheduling system** - it tells you:
- What plays when
- Which ads run at what time
- Who owes you money
- What your announcers should say

---

### The Basic Workflow

```
Advertiser wants to buy airtime
        ↓
Create a Campaign (the deal)
        ↓
Write the Script (what they'll say)
        ↓
Schedule Spots (when it plays)
        ↓
It airs on the radio
        ↓
Bill the advertiser
        ↓
Get paid 💰
```

## Quick Start Guide

### Step 1: Set Up Your Station

Before you can do anything, you need a "Station":

1. Go to **Stations** → **Create New Station**
2. Enter:
   - **Call Sign**: Your station identifier (like "KLOV-FM")
   - **Name**: The friendly name listeners hear
   - **Frequency**: Like "101.5 FM"
3. Save it

> 💡 **Tip**: If you only have one station, you'll mostly work with this one station for everything.

---

### Step 2: Add an Advertiser

Advertisers are people or businesses that pay you to play their commercials.

1. Go to **Advertisers** → **Add New Advertiser**
2. Enter their information:
   - **Name**: Company name
   - **Contact**: Who you talk to
   - **Email/Phone**: How to reach them
3. Save

> 💡 **Think of it like**: Adding a customer to your address book

---

### Step 3: Create Your First Campaign

A **Campaign** is a deal to run commercials for a specific time period.

**Example**: "Joe's Pizza wants to run 30 ads in March"

1. Go to **Campaigns** → **New Campaign**
2. Fill in:
   - **Name**: Something descriptive like "Joe's Pizza - March 2024"
   - **Advertiser**: Select Joe's Pizza
   - **Start Date**: When the campaign begins
   - **End Date**: When it ends
   - **Budget**: What they're paying (helps you track)
3. **Status**: Start as "DRAFT" until ready

---

### Step 4: Write the Script (Copy)

The **script** is what the announcer reads.

1. Go to your Campaign
2. Click **Add Copy/Script**
3. Write what should be said:
   ```
   "Come on down to Joe's Pizza! Family owned since 1985. 
   Mention radio for 20% off your order. Located at 123 Main Street."
   ```
4. Set the **Duration**: How long to read it (usually 15 or 30 seconds)
5. Submit for approval (or approve it yourself)

> 💡 **Tip**: Keep it short and simple. 30 seconds = about 75 words.

---

### Step 5: Schedule Spots

**Spots** are individual times when the ad will play.

1. Go to your Campaign
2. Click **Schedule Spots**
3. For each spot, choose:
   - **Date**: Which day
   - **Time**: What time (like "10:30 AM")
   - **Daypart**: General time category (Morning, Afternoon, Evening)
4. Add as many spots as the campaign requires

> 💡 **What are Dayparts?**
> - **Morning Drive**: 6 AM - 10 AM (expensive - many listeners)
> - **Midday**: 10 AM - 3 PM
> - **Afternoon Drive**: 3 PM - 7 PM (expensive)
> - **Evening**: 7 PM - 12 AM
> - **Overnight**: 12 AM - 6 AM (cheapest)

---

### Step 6: Generate Daily Logs

The **Log** is your daily schedule - everything that plays today.

1. Go to **Daily Logs**
2. Select the date
3. Click **Generate Log**
4. Review it to make sure everything looks right
5. Click **Publish to LibreTime** to send it to your broadcast system

> 💡 **What is LibreTime?** It's the system that actually plays the audio files. LibreLog creates the schedule; LibreTime executes it.

---

### Step 7: Create an Invoice

After ads have aired, bill the advertiser:

1. Go to **Billing** → **Invoices**
2. Click **Generate from Campaign**
3. Select the campaign
4. Set invoice date and due date
5. The system automatically creates an invoice for all aired spots
6. Download or email the invoice

---

### Step 8: Record Payments

When you get paid:

1. Go to **Billing** → **Payments**
2. Click **Record Payment**
3. Select the invoice
4. Enter amount and date
5. Mark the payment method (Check, Credit Card, etc.)
6. Save

The system automatically updates how much is still owed.

## Common Terms Explained

### Advertising Terms

| Term | What It Means | Simple Explanation |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Spot** | One commercial airing | One time an ad plays |
| **Campaign** | Group of spots | The whole deal with an advertiser |
| **Flight** | Campaign duration | How long the campaign runs |
| **Copy** | The script | What the announcer reads |
| **Daypart** | Time of day | Morning, afternoon, evening |
| **Avails** | Available ad time | Empty slots you can sell |
| **Makegood** | Replacement spot | Free spot given if original was missed |

### Technical Terms

| Term | What It Means | Simple Explanation |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Clock** | Hour template | What happens each hour |
| **Grid** | Weekly schedule | Which clock runs each day |
| **Log** | Daily schedule | Everything happening today |
| **Automation** | Computer plays audio | No DJ needed |
| **Voice Track** | Pre-recorded DJ | DJ talks without being live |
| **ID/Legal ID** | Station identification | "You're listening to KLOV 101.5 FM" |

### Billing Terms

| Term | What It Means | Simple Explanation |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Invoice** | Bill sent to advertiser | "You owe us $500" |
| **Payment** | Money received | "They paid $250" |
| **Balance Due** | Money still owed | "They still owe $250" |
| **Net 30** | Due in 30 days | Standard payment terms |
| **Commission** | Sales rep's cut | Percentage salespeople earn |

## Tips for Success

### Do's ✅
- **Plan ahead**: Schedule at least a week in advance
- **Check your logs**: Review daily before publishing
- **Keep scripts short**: 30 seconds max for most spots
- **Track payments**: Follow up on overdue invoices
- **Backup everything**: Export logs and reports regularly

### Don'ts ❌
- **Don't overbook**: Leave room for breaking news/emergencies
- **Don't forget IDs**: Play your Legal ID at the top of each hour
- **Don't skip reconciliation**: Make sure what aired matches what was scheduled
- **Don't ignore aging invoices**: Follow up on unpaid bills promptly
