## Understanding Invoices

### What is an Invoice?

An **invoice** is a bill you send to an advertiser saying:
> "You owe us $X for the commercials that aired."

### Parts of an Invoice

| Part | What It Is | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Invoice Number** | Unique ID | INV-20240315-1234 |
| **Invoice Date** | When you sent it | March 15, 2024 |
| **Due Date** | When payment is due | April 15, 2024 (Net 30) |
| **Line Items** | List of what aired | 20 spots × $50 = $1,000 |
| **Subtotal** | Before tax | $1,000 |
| **Tax** | Sales tax (if applicable) | $80 |
| **Total** | What they owe | $1,080 |
| **Amount Paid** | What they've paid | $540 |
| **Balance Due** | Still owed | $540 |

## Creating an Invoice

### Method 1: Auto-Generate from Campaign

**Best for**: Regular campaign billing

1. Go to **Billing** → **Invoices**
2. Click **Generate from Campaign**
3. Select:
   - Campaign
   - Invoice Date (today)
   - Due Date (usually 30 days out)
4. System automatically:
   - Finds all AIRED spots
   - Calculates costs
   - Creates line items
5. Review and save

> 💡 **Tip**: This is the fastest way and ensures accuracy!

---

### Method 2: Manual Invoice

**Best for**: Special billing situations, adjustments

1. Go to **Billing** → **Invoices** → **New Invoice**
2. Fill in:
   - Campaign
   - Advertiser
   - Invoice and Due dates
3. Add line items manually:
   - Description of service
   - Quantity (number of spots)
   - Unit price (cost per spot)
4. Add tax if applicable
5. Save

## Invoice Status Guide

| Status | Meaning | Action Needed |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| **DRAFT** | Still working on it | Finish and send |
| **SENT** | Emailed/mailed to advertiser | Wait for payment |
| **PARTIALLY PAID** | Some money received | Follow up for rest |
| **PAID** | Fully paid | Nothing - you're done! |
| **OVERDUE** | Past due date | Contact advertiser |
| **CANCELLED** | Voided | Don't collect |

## Recording Payments

### When a Payment Arrives

1. Go to **Billing** → **Payments**
2. Click **Record Payment**
3. Select the invoice being paid
4. Enter:
   - **Amount**: How much they paid
   - **Payment Date**: When you received it
   - **Payment Method**:
     - Check
     - Credit Card
     - Cash
     - ACH/Bank Transfer
   - **Reference Number**: Check number, transaction ID
5. Add any notes
6. Save

### What Happens Automatically

The system:
- ✅ Updates the invoice balance
- ✅ Changes status to PAID or PARTIALLY PAID
- ✅ Records payment history
- ✅ Updates advertiser's total balance

### For One Invoice

1. Open the invoice

3. See all payments with dates and amounts

### For Date Range

1. Go to **Billing** → **Payments**
2. Set date range (e.g., "March 1-31, 2024")

## Tracking Outstanding Balances

### See What Advertisers Owe

1. Go to **Billing** → **Invoices** → **Overdue**
2. Or check individual advertiser:
   - Go to Advertiser profile
   - View "Outstanding Balance"

### Aging Report

Shows how long invoices have been unpaid:
- **Current**: Due now or future
- **1-30 Days**: Slightly late
- **31-60 Days**: Needs follow-up
- **60+ Days**: Serious collection needed

## Best Practices

### Invoicing
- ✅ **Invoice promptly**: Within 1 week of month-end
- ✅ **Due date**: Standard is Net 30 (30 days)
- ✅ **Email AND mail**: Some advertisers need paper
- ✅ **Keep copies**: Save PDFs of all invoices
- ✅ **Number sequentially**: Helps with organization

### Payment Tracking
- ✅ **Record same day**: Don't wait - record when received
- ✅ **Check reference numbers**: Match to bank deposits
- ✅ **Follow up at 30 days**: Friendly reminder
- ✅ **Escalate at 60 days**: More serious follow-up
- ✅ **Review monthly**: Know who owes what

### Communication
- ✅ **Payment terms upfront**: Tell advertisers Net 30 before starting
- ✅ **Late fee policy**: Decide if/when you charge late fees
- ✅ **Thank you notes**: For prompt payment
- ✅ **Regular statements**: Monthly account summaries

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: "Can't generate invoice - no aired spots"
**Solution**: 
- Check that spots are marked as AIRED
- Verify you're selecting the right date range
- Spots must air BEFORE you can bill them

### Problem: "Invoice total is wrong"
**Solution**:
- Check line items for correct quantities
- Verify unit prices match your rates
- Look for spots that shouldn't be included
- Edit the invoice to fix errors

### Problem: "Advertiser paid wrong amount"
**Solution**:
- Record the payment as received
- Create a new invoice for the remaining balance
- Contact advertiser about the discrepancy

### Problem: "Need to refund advertiser"
**Solution**:
- Create a credit memo (negative invoice)
- Or record a payment with negative amount
- Consult your accountant on best method

## Tax Considerations

> ⚠️ **Important**: Tax rules vary by location. Consult a local accountant!

### Common Scenarios

**Sales Tax**:
- Some states require sales tax on advertising
- Usually based on where the station is located
- Not where the advertiser is located

**Tax-Exempt Clients**:
- Non-profits may be tax-exempt
- Get their tax-exempt certificate
- Don't charge them tax

### Setting Tax Rate

1. When creating invoice
2. Set tax rate (e.g., 8.25% = 0.0825)
3. System calculates automatically

## FAQ

**Q: When should I invoice?**  
A: Usually monthly, after spots have aired. Don't bill for future spots!

**Q: What if an advertiser doesn't pay?**  
A: 
1. Friendly reminder at 30 days
2. Formal notice at 60 days  
3. Collection agency at 90+ days
4. Stop running their ads until paid

**Q: Can I invoice for makegoods?**  
A: No - makegoods are free replacements for missed spots.

**Q: How do I handle deposits?**  
A: 
- Record as payment when received
- Apply to invoice when generated
- Or create a "prepayment" invoice

**Q: What about commissions for sales reps?**  
A: Usually calculated on paid invoices, not just billed amounts. Check with sales manager.
