# Campaign Management Help

> **What is a Campaign?**  
> A campaign is a deal between you and an advertiser to run commercials over a specific time period.

## Campaign Lifecycle

```
DRAFT → PENDING APPROVAL → ACTIVE → COMPLETED
   ↓           ↓              ↓         ↓
 Planning  Waiting for    Running    Done!
           confirmation
```

---

## Creating a Campaign - Step by Step

### Step 1: Campaign Details

**Name**: Give it a descriptive name
- ✅ Good: "Joe's Pizza - March Madness 2024"
- ❌ Bad: "Campaign #1"

**Why it matters**: When you have 20 campaigns running, you'll thank yourself for clear names.

---

### Step 2: Set Your Dates

**Start Date**: When the first ad can air
**End Date**: When the campaign ends

> 💡 **Important**: The campaign won't show spots outside these dates!

**Example Timeline**:
- Start: March 1, 2024
- End: March 31, 2024
- Duration: 1 month

---

### Step 3: Budget and Spots

**Total Spots**: How many times will the ad play?

**Budget**: What the advertiser is paying

**Quick Math**:
```
If charging $50 per spot
And running 20 spots
Total Budget = $1,000
```

## Campaign Status Guide

| Status | What It Means | When to Use |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| **DRAFT** | Still planning | You're working on it |
| **PENDING APPROVAL** | Waiting for go-ahead | Advertiser hasn't confirmed yet |
| **ACTIVE** | Running now | Ads are being scheduled |
| **PAUSED** | Temporarily stopped | Advertiser asked to hold |
| **COMPLETED** | Finished | All spots have aired |
| **CANCELLED** | Won't run | Deal fell through |

## Scheduling Spots Within a Campaign

### What is a Spot?

A **spot** is ONE time an ad plays.

**Example**: If an ad plays at 9:00 AM, 12:00 PM, and 5:00 PM, that's 3 spots.

---

### How to Schedule Spots

1. Open your campaign
2. Click "Schedule Spots"
3. For each spot, fill in:

#### Date and Time
- **Scheduled Date**: Which day (March 15, 2024)
- **Scheduled Time**: What time (09:00:00 = 9:00 AM)

#### Daypart
Select the general time category:
- **Morning Drive** (6 AM - 10 AM): Rush hour, many listeners = higher price
- **Midday** (10 AM - 3 PM): Work hours
- **Afternoon Drive** (3 PM - 7 PM): Rush hour, many listeners = higher price
- **Evening** (7 PM - 12 AM): After work
- **Overnight** (12 AM - 6 AM): Few listeners = lower price

#### Break Position
Where in the commercial break:
- **Position 1**: First ad (best memory)
- **Position 2**: Second ad
- **Position 3+**: Later ads

> 💡 **Tip**: Position 1 costs more but gets better recall

## Spot Status Tracking

| Status | Meaning | Next Step |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| **SCHEDULED** | On the schedule | Wait for air date |
| **CONFIRMED** | Double-checked | Ready to air |
| **AIRED** | Already played | Bill for it |
| **MISSED** | Didn't air | Schedule makegood |
| **MAKEGOOD SCHEDULED** | Replacement set | Monitor it |

## Creating Makegoods

### What is a Makegood?

If an ad **didn't air** when scheduled (technical problem, emergency programming, etc.), you owe the advertiser a **free replacement spot**.

### How to Create a Makegood

1. Find the missed spot
2. Click "Create Makegood"
3. Choose new date and time
4. The system:
   - Marks original as "MISSED"
   - Creates new spot as "MAKEGOOD_SCHEDULED"
   - Links them together
5. No charge for makegoods!

> 💡 **Industry Standard**: Always give makegoods for missed spots. It builds trust.

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: "Can't schedule spot - date outside campaign range"
**Solution**: The spot date must be between campaign start and end dates.

### Problem: "Campaign shows 0 spots aired"
**Solution**: 
- Check that spots are marked as AIRED
- Verify spots actually happened (check logs)
- Make sure spots weren't cancelled

### Problem: "Advertiser wants to cancel"
**Solution**:
1. Change campaign status to CANCELLED
2. Note: You may still bill for spots that already aired
3. Discuss refund policy with your manager

## Best Practices

### Scheduling
- ✅ Spread spots throughout the week (don't bunch them)
- ✅ Mix dayparts for better coverage
- ✅ Don't schedule back-to-back (leave space between spots)
- ✅ Confirm scripts before scheduling

### Communication
- ✅ Send weekly reports to advertisers
- ✅ Notify advertisers of any missed spots immediately
- ✅ Get written approval for schedule changes
- ✅ Confirm start dates before launching

### Organization
- ✅ Use consistent naming: "[Advertiser] - [Promotion] - [Month Year]"
- ✅ Add notes for special instructions
- ✅ Set reminders for campaign end dates
- ✅ Review upcoming expirations weekly

## FAQ

**Q: Can I change a spot after it's scheduled?**  
A: Yes, if it hasn't aired yet. Just edit the spot details.

**Q: What if an advertiser wants to add more spots?**  
A: You can add spots to an active campaign anytime within the date range.

**Q: How do I know if a spot actually aired?**  
A: Check the spot status (should be "AIRED") and review your daily reconciliation report.

**Q: Can I schedule the same ad multiple times per day?**  
A: Yes, but space them out. Don't run the same ad back-to-back.

**Q: What's the difference between a campaign and an order?**  
A: Think of an order as the paperwork, and a campaign as the active scheduling. Some stations use them interchangeably.
